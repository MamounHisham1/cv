# Product

## Register

split

## Users

Job seekers and working professionals who want to build ATS-optimized resumes and prepare for interviews. They come to SeratyAI when they need a polished CV fast or want to practice with an AI interviewer. Context: typically at home on a laptop, preparing for applications, often balancing time pressure with the desire for quality output. They range from early-career to senior professionals across industries.

## Product Purpose

AI-powered career toolkit: build professional CVs, evaluate them against ATS criteria, and practice mock interviews with voice-based AI. The product saves time, increases interview callback rates, and builds confidence. Success looks like a user creating a polished CV in under 15 minutes, getting actionable improvement feedback, and completing a realistic interview practice session.

## Brand Personality

Quietly confident. The interface speaks with expert precision, not hype. Three words: precise, composed, capable. The emotional goal is reassurance through competence: the user should feel they are using a tool built by people who understand hiring, not a gimmick. Tone is direct and helpful. No exclamation marks selling excitement. No urgency tricks.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: white/cream backgrounds, default blue accent, identical card grids. The overused "modern SaaS" look that could be any product.
- Glassmorphism overload: blurry cards, frosted-glass-everywhere, decorative backdrop-blur as the default surface treatment. Use it sparingly, not as identity.
- Marketplace clutter: too many competing colors, gradient badges, star ratings scattered everywhere, promotional banners. Signal noise.
- Corporate boring: cold enterprise aesthetics, stock-photo handshakes, generic business language, templated layouts that feel like internal tools.

## Design Principles

1. Confidence through restraint. Every element earns its place. If removing it changes nothing, remove it. The interface should feel intentionally sparse, not accidentally empty.
2. Surface serves function. Decorative elements exist only when they reinforce the user's task. Glassmorphism, gradients, and animations are tools, not defaults. Use them when they add clarity or delight, never as wallpaper.
3. Hierarchy does the work. Use scale, weight, and spacing contrast to guide attention. Avoid relying on color alone to communicate importance. A well-structured layout needs minimal decoration.
4. Expert tone in every word. Copy is direct, specific, and free of filler. No "seamlessly", no "unlock your potential", no marketing fluff inside the app. The landing page can sell; the product should deliver.
5. Dark by intention. The dark theme is a deliberate choice for focus and professionalism, not a trend. Keep zinc-950 as the base, emerald as the controlled accent. Light mode exists but dark leads.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target enforced. Keep contrast ratios reasonable, ensure keyboard navigability, and avoid relying solely on color to convey meaning.
